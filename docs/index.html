Debating a Winning Tune

Jamie Czerwinski —August 14, 2015

A new study suggests that candidates with deep voices are more likely to win over voters in elections. How do Canada's current candidates stack up?

Here are (smoothed) probability density plots of each leaders voice pitch through the first twenty minutes of the 2015 Macleans National Leaders Debate.

Judging by voice depth alone, Mulcair won. Also, notice the dispersion of the distributions. Elizabeth May seemed the most genuine, and it shows in her range of vocal expressiveness. Similarly, Mulcair seemed the most rehearsed, and it showed in his lack of range.

Alberta Population Pyramid

Jamie Czerwinski —February 3, 2014

Alberta's population by age group, gender, and year.

A couple of observations. First, from 1975 until recently, its been all about the Baby Boomers. They have really dominated demographically.

But look at the last few years. Alberta attracts a lot of workers, most of whom are relatively young. As of 2012, 25-35 year olds outnumber 45-55 year olds.

Times have changed.
